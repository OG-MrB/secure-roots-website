Our Programs Get Involved

The Digital Divide Is Real — And It's in Our Backyard

In Los Angeles County's Second Supervisorial District — Compton, Inglewood, Willowbrook, Watts, South LA, Baldwin Hills, Leimert Park, and surrounding communities — the digital divide isn't an abstract concept. It's a daily reality that limits opportunity, deepens inequality, and leaves our most vulnerable neighbors behind.

416,000+ LA County households lack internet access
9.7% of households have no internet subscription
4.6% of households have no computer at all
2x Disproportionate impact on Black and Latino communities

Youth without digital skills face limited career options. Seniors face isolation and vulnerability to online scams. But this is a solvable problem — and we're solving it.

Our Programs

Secure Futures

Youth Digital Pathways

A prevention and intervention program for at-risk youth ages 12–18, combining cybersecurity training, coding workshops, AI literacy, one-on-one mentoring, and life skills development. We give young people the tools to build careers in tech — not just survive, but thrive.

Digital Roots

Intergenerational Tech Literacy

An intergenerational program where trained youth serve as Digital Assistants for seniors, teaching smartphone basics, online safety, telehealth access, and digital communication skills. Two generations learning together, lifting each other up.

Community Tech Day

Free Public Events

Free, full-day community events providing hands-on technology education for all ages — coding workshops for kids, cybersecurity awareness for families, digital skills for job seekers, and one-on-one tech help desks. Open to all Second District residents.

Rooted in the Community We Serve

Secure Roots is based in LA County's Second Supervisorial District because this is our community. We understand the challenges our neighbors face because we face them too. We bring real-world cybersecurity expertise directly to the people who need it most — with programming designed for the populations hardest hit by the digital divide.
